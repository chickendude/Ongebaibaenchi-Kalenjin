**Neko ak bandek** – Goats and maize

### Dialog

| Speaker       | Kalenjin                                                | English                                                                     |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Belyon | Kibet, ngoro neko? Iyoe nee en yon, kakoba neko koam bandekab bik. | Kibet, where are the goats? What are you doing there, the goats have gone to eat people's maize. |
| Kibet | Momi bandek. Kokikes amut tugul ak kokibor. Mobek kityo ak machakwek momi bandiat agot. | There's no maize. All was harvested and shelled yesterday. Stalks and cobs only not even a single maize. |
| Belyon | Aikwek wee, kokikes koit tembwet yun i? Momwowan inendet Kipchamba. Tos kokes morik anan ko kibarua? Mangen kit ne ibwote chichon. | Oh my, was it harvested up to the valley there? Kipchamba himself didn't tell me. Did the group harvest or was it paid contract? I don't know what that person is thinking. |
| Kibet | Ko kibarua. Kokes bik somok bandek tugul en kasarta ne nwach. Kotinye bichoto kora chebore ne koboren bandek. | It was a paid contract. Three people harvested the whole maize in a short time. They also had sheller machine they shelled the maize. |
| Belyon | Tos kokes chechang anan che tuten? Ayoni ko chechang amun mbaret inoni ko wo missing. | Did he harvest a lot or a little? I believe it's a lot because this land is very big |
| Kibet | Makes chechang missing. Ibwote kemeut ne kimiten en arawetab Mut. Morut komie bandek. | He didn't harvest alot. Do you remember the drought which was there in the month of may. Maize didn't grow well. |
| Belyon | Amoche asom roret ayagen neko chechuk. Asome ng'o? | I want to request the harvested land to graze my goats. Who will I borrow? |
| Kibet | Som werinyin ne ming'in anan isom boiyot inendet. | Request his younger son or the old man himself. |
| Belyon | Kiitu ou werinyin ne ming'in en bitonin? | When did his youngest son arrive from overseas? |
| Kibet | Koitu kemoi amut. Ngandan pirto met inyoru isom. Kaigai mang'ok kou werit ne wo. | He arrived yesterday night. Even though he's dizzy meet and borrow. He's better, not selfish like the elder son. |
| Belyon | Iman. Abun ano? | True, where do I pass? |
| Kibet | Bun roret kwenet ak ichakte. | Pass through the harvested land and hurry. |

kai1 gai1 \= better  
kai3 gai1 \= please  
i3 gai1 gai13 \= to beg

### Vocabulary

1. **artet** / **neko** – goat
2. **bandiat / bandek** – (piece of) corn, maize
3. **kakoba** – they've already gone
    1. **ui** (mid-falling tone) – to go
    2. **wendi** – going (sg)
    3. **bendi** – going (pl)
4. **mopchot** / **mobek** – corn stalk
5. **machakwat** / **machakwek** – corn cob (without kernels)
    1. **bandiat** / **bandek** – corn with the kernels on it
6. **kes** – to harvest
7. **bor** – to shell
8. **aikwek wee** – a phrase expressing surprise, similar to "oh my\!"
9. **tembwet** – valley
    1. also **legem** / **lekem**
10. **bwat** – to think
11. **morik** – community work  
    *Morik* is a group where people help each other. Today we'll come and do work at your place, next we'll go to my place, and so on.
12. **kibarua** – a paid job, short contract (Swahili loan)
13. **chebore** – sheller machine
14. **yan** – believe / agree
15. **kemeut** – drought
16. **rut** – to grow
    1. **rut** – to pierce or inject, but pronunciation differs
17. **som** – request / borrow
18. **roret** – land where maize harvesting has just been completed
19. **agot** – even
20. **tu** – to arrive
21. **bitet** – other side of the river
22. **ngandan** – even though  
    also **nganon**
23. **pirten** – to shake
24. **metit** – head
25. **pirto met**  – in this case, kalenjin we say pirto met, he is shaking the head. we know when someone is dizzy keeps shaking the head and kalenjin say pirto met
26. **nyorun** – to get / to catch
    1. also **nam**
27. **ng'ok** – selfish
28. **bun** – to pass through
29. **kwenet** – middle
30. **chakte(n)** – hurry

### Grammar

1\. Kakoba - "ko" shows the action is already done. Kakosus Michael bendo, kakopir Kanyinge lakwet. Kakonyebut Lucas maiwek.  
3\. a. Kit - thing   
b. Chichon - shortened from "chito non" that person   
4\. a. Bichoto - shortened from "bik choto" those people   
b. Kora - again, also   
5\. "ko" no real meaning. Just to make sentence flow well. It is also correct to say "ayoni chechang amun mbaret inoni ko wo missing" (shall explain in our next class how it works)  
6\. Yes, "ko" used to form adverbs.
