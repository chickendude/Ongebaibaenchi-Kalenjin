## Lesson 10

**Moning’otiot ak kwondo** – Husband and wife

[Audio](audio/10%20Moning'otiot.mp3)

### Dialog

| Speaker       | Kalenjin                                                | English                                                                     |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Moning’otiot | Iyo, nyon yat kot. Kaagurin saisiek chechang menyone iyate kot. Iimin nee rani? | Iyo, come open the house. I have called you several hours, but you’re not coming to open the house. What’s disturbing you? |
| Kwondo | Iyobu ano saisyek chebo kemoi. Mayote kot. | Where are you coming from at these hours of the night? I will not open the house. |
| Moning’otiot | Yatwan keng’alal. Roboni, twonen ingoroik chechuk. Kayai abotan, kaitit. | Open for me, (so) we (can) talk. It’s raining, my clothes are wet. It has made me shiver, it's cold. |
| Kwondo | Mamache anai, kemiten ano? | I don’t care ("I don't want to know"), where were you? |
| Moning’otiot | Chelang’at. Yatwan kot kaigai asom. U ya metit en komet rani. | Chelang’at. Please open the house for me I beg. It’s like your mother's head is bad today. |
| Chelangat | Achicha, piron kametnyun ndo ayat kot. | No, my mother will beat me if I open the house. |
| Kwondo | Chelang’at\! Meyom iyat kot, bagach konut kopir ropta en bii. | Chelangat\! Don’t try open the house, leave your father to be rained on outside. |
| Moning’otiot | Aikwek wee, Kipsigis che kisichan. Obot Chelang’at ni ki mie keny. | Kipsigis who gave birth to me. Chelangat’s mother who was good long time ago. |
| Chelang’at | Istenkee en kurgat. Mwei ak iwe iunykee amun nyone iyo ak bek che bele bik\! | Get away from the door. Run and go hide, because mom is coming with hot water (water which burns people)\! |
| Kwondo | Sis lakwani. Amache akoten konut amun kikoten muguleldanyun en kasitab mut. | Be quiet, (this) child. I want to injure your father because he injured my heart on Friday |
| Chelangat | Kilyan? Inereche ra missing. Imuche neregekuk koyo bendab sigiriet. | What happened? You’re very angry today. Your anger can cook donkeys’ meat |
| Kwondo | Kianyoru kimi ak chepkeleliot kot orit ichegen. | I met him with a lady inside the house alone. |
| Chelangat | Iyo, ibwate che samis kityo. Nyolu iwal kabwatutik cheguk che samis. | Mother, you only think bad. You should change your bad thoughts. |

### Vocabulary

1. **ichegen, icheken** – alone  
   From “ichek” – “them”. When **-en** is added, the meaning changes to alone.
2. **orit** – inside
3. **sis** – keep quiet
    1. **sisingei, sisingee** – quiet yourself
4. **koyo** – to cook – “yo” cook
5. **sigiriet** – donkey
6. **koten** – to injure
7. **mwei** – Run away  
   used when there is danger or running away from danger, also when you did something bad and ran away we say “mwei” e.g.:
    1. **Mwei amun nyone ng’etundo\!** – Run because the lion is coming\!
    2. **Kinyer lakwet ak komwei.** – He raped the child and ran away.
8. **istenkee, istengee** – get out /shift
9. **kurgat** – door  
   Also:
    1. **musereita** – door
10. **iunykee** – hide yourself
    1. uny – to hide
11. **iyo** – Mother (name Kipsigis call their mother)
12. **bele** – is burning
    1. **bel** – to burn, roast
    2. **bel bendo** – roast the meat
    3. **bele bik kawek** – the coffee is hot
13. **meyom** – don’t try
14. **twonen** – They’re  wet
    1. **twon** – wet
    2. **twon ingoriet** – the cloth is wet
    3. **twonen ingoroik** – the clothes are wet
15. **abotan** – I shiver – From the word “botan” means ‘shiver’
16. **kayai** – It has made
    1. yai – to do / make
17. **kaitit** – it’s cold
18. **iimin** – it’s disturbing you
    1. **iim** – to disturb
19. **iyo** – May I come in?  
    In Africa, the doors didn’t have doors to knock. So Kipsigis stand at the door and say "iyo" and wait for the response to be allowed to get in. The pronunciation is different from the name for Mother.
20. **iyobu** – Where are you from?
    1. yobu – to come from
21. **obot** – Mother of (same as ‘kametab’)
22. **ibwate** – you’re thinking
    1. bwat – to think
23. **samis** – bad
24. **kabwatutik** – thoughts
25. **iwal** – you change
    1. **wal** – to change
